# Content Writing Guidelines: Israeli Election App

This document defines the editorial standards, tone of voice, bilingual localization guidelines, and content structure for the Israeli Election App. These guidelines ensure that all written text is direct, factually correct, and objective—focusing on reality without compromise.

---

## 1. Core Editorial Philosophy

*   **Reality-First Objectivity**: Objectivity does not mean neutral dilution or hiding uncomfortable truths. The app must present facts as they are. If a party leader is criminally indicted, convicted, or under active investigation, state it clearly. Do not shy away from reality to avoid controversy or offense.
*   **Equal Scrutiny**: While we present reality without sugarcoating, all parties, leaders, and platforms must be weighed on equal grounds. Apply the exact same standard of factual proof and journalistic rigor to every political entity. Zero selective bias.
*   **Fact-Based Integrity**: All platform summaries, historical actions, and candidate statements must be verified by official documents, court rulings, or reliable public record. Avoid rumors or unsubstantiated claims.

---

## 2. Tone and Voice

We write with a **direct, fearless, and analytical** tone.

*   **Correct and Direct**: Do not use euphemisms. If a policy is a "deportation policy," call it that. If a candidate has been "convicted of supporting a terror organization," state it as a fact.
*   **Unbiased but Unflinching**: Do not soften hard realities to appear "balanced." Balance means presenting all sides of a debate fairly, not hiding one side's negative facts to match the other.
*   **No Editorializing**: State the facts and let the user draw conclusions. Do not use loaded adjectives to guide the user's emotion (e.g., write *"Party X proposed a budget cut of 5%"* rather than *"Party X proposed a devastating budget cut"*).

---

## 3. Bilingual Copywriting (Hebrew & English)

To maintain content parity across Hebrew and English:

*   **Meaning Equivalence**: Ensure that the direct and unflinching tone translates perfectly. Do not soften the English translation of a Hebrew term, or vice versa, to suit different cultural expectations.
*   **Length & Density**: Hebrew is typically more concise than English. When writing in English, be highly economical with words to ensure the text remains punchy and matches the informational density of the Hebrew version.
*   **Terminology Mapping**: Use precise political and legal translations (e.g., "Indictment" = "כתב אישום", "Conviction" = "הרשעה").
*   **Hebrew Gender Neutrality**: In Hebrew, use plural language (פנייה בלשון רבים) when addressing the user to remain as gender-neutral as possible. For example, use *"גלו איפה אתם עומדים"* (Discover where you stand) rather than *"גלה איפה אתה עומד"* (singular masculine).

---

## 4. Quiz Question Formulation

Quiz questions must be neutral but realistic, focusing on real political divides.

*   **Realistic Framing**: Address the core of the debate directly. Do not hide the controversial elements of a policy.
*   **Unbiased Options**: Ensure choice answers are written with the same tone, detail, and structure. If one option details a policy's rationale, the opposing option must detail its rationale with equal factual weight.
*   **Leading Language Check**: Avoid words that imply a correct answer. Focus on the core conflict (e.g., *"Should the state recognize civil marriages?"* rather than *"Should the state grant citizens the freedom to marry civilly?"*).

---

## 5. Party Platform Agendas & Summaries

When summarizing where a user aligns or misaligns with a party on the results screen:

*   **Factual Platform Summaries**: Summarize party platforms based on their official bills, manifestos, and voting records.
*   **Represent Conflict Directly**: If a user aligns with a party on economy but strongly clashes on state-and-religion, state the mismatch directly without wrapping it in soft language.
